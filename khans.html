    Khans of Kazakh Khanate

    All Kazakh Khans are descendants of Genghis Khan, that is, they belong to the Genghis Khan dynasty. The Kazakh Khanate was one of the most powerful countries of its time. Under kasym Khan, the number of soldiers in the Kazakh army reached 300 thousand, and under Yessim Khan, their number exceeded 400 thousand. These Khans were engaged not only in politics, but also in military affairs and legislation.

    In the era of kasym Khan, collections of laws were published: "Kaska Zhol", "Old Road" under Yessim Khan, and "Zheti zhargy" in the time of Tauke Khan.

    The residences of the Kazakh Khans were located in different places in different times. During the reign of kasym Khan, the residence was located in the recently conquered city of Sarayshyk. In 1599, Yessim Khan moved the residence to the Big Aksaray and small Aksaray rooms of the mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasawi in Turkestan.

    Several Kazakh Khans were buried in the same mausoleum. And Yessim Khan himself has his own mausoleum in Turkestan. Kasym Khan has a mausoleum in Sarayshyk.
